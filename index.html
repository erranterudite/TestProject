Feeling down or in a funk? Here are several ways to hack into happiness. These techniques are drawn from positive psychology and can change your mood from funk to fulfilled

Gratitude Journal

Studies have shown that cultivating gratitude is a good way to improve your mood, energy, and relationships. Start a gratitude journal today, either the old fashioned way with a paper and pen, or through a website that allows you to share your gratitude with others: http://gratefulness.org/

Random Acts of Kindness

Find one unexpected, kind thing to do for someone today and do it. Be creative and have some fun with it. Here are a few examples:

  • Feed someone’s parking meter
  • Pay someone a compliment
  • Buy someone a cup of coffee
  • Hold a door
  • Smile and say hello
  • Call a friend
  • Pay for the next person's coffee
  • Hand out a paper hearts that say "Have a Nice Day!"
  • Write nice comments on comment cards in restaurants
  • Say please and thank you!
  • Text your friends just to say hello
    • Smile!!
  • Let someone go ahead of you in grocery line (or any line)
  • Pick up a piece of litter
  • Contribute to a food bank Donate
  • Share a music playlist with a friend – particularly one that’s inspiring / upbeat!
  • Write uplifting notes on someone’s door board
  • Attend a campus event – support your fellow students in their hard work!

Mindful Moment

In our fast paced world, we don’t often slow down to fully appreciate and be present in the moment. Choose an activity and completely focus on this activity as slowly as you can. For example, savoring what you eat. Notice the shape, size, and color of a piece of fruit. How it feels when you hold it, what it tastes like on your tongue and teeth when you chew. What it sounds like when you bite into it. Fully engage all of your senses on the experience of savoring that food. Practice a mindful moment each day when you spent time, quietly savoring something. Slow down!

Tap Your Senses

  • Have a special treat (chocolate, hot tea, cold drink) mindfully. Enjoy each sip, bite, etc.
  • Walk outside and smell the fresh air, light a candle or incense, use a new lotion or scent
  • Look at nature, enjoy art, look at flowers, walk in your favorite part of town
  • Take a bubble bath, pet an animal, swim in a pool, use comfortable sheets on your bed
  • Listen to soothing music, use water noises in your home, be mindful of noises around you

Improve the situation

  • We can’t always change the situation, but we can learn to make ourselves feel better about it.
  • Imagine feeling more in control where you are. What would this look like? How would it be different?
  • Search for meaning in the struggle (lemons ‡ lemonade)
  • Seek support from a higher power, God, or your own wise self
  • What do you find relaxing? Is it healthy? Engage in it!
  • Simply focus on participating in one activity at a time – stay focused on the present.
  • Take a small vacation, whether it’s a walk around the block or a mini vacation in your head!
  • Encourage yourself! Talk with a friend, parent, teacher, therapist, or doctor who supports you!

Accept the Situation

  • What do you enjoy? Go do something!
  • Give back! Volunteer, babysit, do something nice for another person.
  • Compare where you are now with where you were before. Are you better?
  • Connect with a different emotion – read, watch a movie, listen to music
  • For just a moment - you can always come back to it when you’re more able.
  • Count to 10, focus on something you enjoy - distract yourself so that you center
